The article is about a couple, Mohammad Riza Ahmad Hambadley and Wan Ziraiza Wan Ismail, who are facing 78 charges in the Sessions Court of Ipoh relating to a fraud case worth RM52.8 million that occurred from 2016 to 2024. The couple is charged under various sections of the Anti-Money Laundering, Anti-Terrorism Financing and Proceeds from Unlawful Activities Act 2001 and the Financial Services Act 2013 for activities related to a charity organization, Amal Kita Kinta Perak.

The charges under the Financial Services Act involve receiving deposits without a valid license, which is allegedly in violation of Section 10 of the act. The activities occurred between August 16, 2017, and July 15, 2023, at No. 11A, Lorong Dewangsa 2, Batu Gajah, Kinta District.

The charges under the Financial Services Act carry a maximum penalty of up to 10 years in prison or a fine not exceeding RM50 million, or both. This case is being prosecuted by representatives from the Malaysian Anti-Corruption Commission (MACC), the Securities Commission Malaysia (SCM), and Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM). The couple has been represented by lawyers Datuk Yaacob Md Sam and Md Yunos Shariff.

The court set a bail of RM100,000 for each defendant, with an additional condition that they should not interfere with the prosecution witnesses and must report to the nearby police station once a month between 1st and 5th of every month. The next mention date is scheduled for November 4.

In relation to Bank Negara Malaysia, this case highlights its role in collaborating with other agencies to enforce financial regulations and combat fraudulent activities.
