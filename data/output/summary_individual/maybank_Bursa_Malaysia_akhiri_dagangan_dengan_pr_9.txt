Maybank's shares increased by 2 sen to RM8.96 in today's trading session, contributing to the growth of FTSE Bursa Malaysia KLCI (FBM KLCI). Despite a negative market environment with losers outnumbering winners, the local bourse experienced higher trading volume and value compared to Wednesday, according to Rakuten Trade Sdn Bhd's Vice President of Research, Thong Pak Leng. He suggested that FBM KLCI is likely to remain stable with fluctuations between 1,535 and 1,545 until Friday. Other significant gains were reported by Telekom Malaysia, Public Bank, IHH Healthcare, and Cypark. The FBM ACE, FBM Emas Syariah, FBM Emas, FBMT 100, and FBM 70 indices also showed growth.
