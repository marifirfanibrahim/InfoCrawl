The article reports on a couple, Mohammad Riza Ahmad Hambadley and Wan Ziraiza Wan Ismail, being charged with 75 counts of fraud worth approximately RM52.8 million in the Ipoh Sessions Court. The charges, which span from 2016 to 2024, are related to a scam involving the Foundation Amal Kita Kinta Perak, where they allegedly accepted deposits without proper licenses, violating Section 4(1)(b) of the Anti-Money Laundering, Anti-Terrorism Financing and Proceeds from Unlawful Activities Act 2001.

The couple, who are directors of Foundation Amal Kita Kinta Perak, face charges under Section 137(1) of the Financial Services Act 2013 for their roles in two schemes: the Program Ayam Kampung Pertubuhan Amal Kita Kinta and Program Induk Kambing Baka Shami Pertubuhan Amal Kita Kinta. These activities occurred between August 16, 2017, and July 15, 2023, at No. 11A, Lorong Dewangsa 2 in Batu Gajah within the Kinta district.

Each defendant may face up to 10 years in prison or a fine of up to RM50 million, or both. Additionally, charges have been brought against them under Section 3(2)(b) of the Securities Commission Act 2016 for offenses committed between March 3, 2017, and January 7, 2021.

The prosecution is being led by Deputy Public Prosecutor Steve Chin Yun Cheong, SSM Officer Fazarul Ezhar Mohd Mokhtar and Nurul Nabila Azmi, as well as Bank Negara Malaysia Officers Muhamad Atif Abdullah and Harvind Raja Gopal. The defendants are represented by lawyers Datuk Yaacob Md Sam and Md Yunos Shariff.

The court has set a bond of RM100,000 for each defendant with an additional condition that they should not harass witnesses or report themselves to the police station monthly between 1st and 5th of every month.
